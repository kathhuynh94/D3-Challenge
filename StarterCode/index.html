Correlations Discovered Between Poverty, Age, and Income against Lack of Healthcare, Smokers, and Obesity

Poverty vs. Healthcare: The states that have the most poverty rates also have a correlation with lacking the most healthcare.

Age vs Healthcare: Those that are around the ages of 38-40 are on average lacking healthcare.

Income vs. Healthcare: The higher your income, the lower the lack of healthcare is. Therefore showing a negative trend line.

Poverty vs. Smokers: The higher the poverty rate, the more smokers there are.

Age vs. Smokers: The average age range of smokers are those who are in between their late 30s and early 40s. The data does not show a division between male or female smoker percentages.

Income vs. Smokers: The lower the income, the higher the percentage of people smoking.

Poverty vs. Obesity: The higher obesity rates seem to come from states that have the highest poverty percentage.

Age vs. Obesity: The obesity rates are more evident for the states with people that average 38 years old.

Income vs. Obesity: At a similar trend to poverty and obesity, the higher obesity rates are from states with the lowest income.
